About me

When I was 24, I made a pivotal decision to change my career from accounting to software engineering . After completing my SPM (Sijil Pelajaran Malaysia), I was uncertain about my future career path, so I chose accounting, a field widely regarded as having plentiful job opportunities. However, after two years, I realized that I did not enjoy the profession and lacked a clear future career plan. This led me to a period of self-reflection to better understand my interests and desires. In a self-directed effort, with no one to guide me, I decided to learn programming. After researching on Google and various forums, I learned that the C language is challenging and not particularly beginner-friendly. However, mastering it can be immensely beneficial, as it establishes a strong foundation for understanding the logic and concepts that are crucial for more advanced programming. Although I've since forgotten much of it, that foundational knowledge greatly assisted me in learning other later, higher-level programming languages such as Java and Python. Therefore, in 2019, I left the field of accounting to join the expansive world of software engineering, immersing myself in unfamiliar subjects and pursuing cutting-edge technology.
